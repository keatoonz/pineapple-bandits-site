Bio

Pineapple Bandits are what happens when four musicians get tired of polite music. Based in Sheffield and spiritually located somewhere between Corporation and an existential crisis, they make alt-rock for people who want to feel slightly attacked, but in a good way.

Band Members

  • Lauren – Vocals
  • Kane – Vocals / Bass
  • Byron – Guitar
  • Keaton – Drums

About

Pineapple Bandits write music that holds nothing back. Built from tension, noise, and catharsis, their sound speaks to people who feel everything all at once. If you're trying to keep it together or let it all out, this band gives you something to hang onto.

The four members met while studying music in Sheffield. What started as a shared drive to create something personal has become a tightly formed unit that produces every part of their sound in-house.

The band is influenced by artists like Bring Me The Horizon, Hot Milk, and Spiritbox, but their approach isn't tied to genre. Their upcoming EP, written and recorded by the band in a mix of university studios and home setups, blends modern metal production with punk attitude and emotional transparency. Songs aren't constructed, they're felt out loud, often reworked mid-session to stay honest.

Live, the band avoids backing tracks and pre-recorded safety nets. Their shows run on connection, intensity, and control. They've built a reputation for raw but precise performances that feel like an extension of the studio rather than a compromise of it. The band takes pride in rehearsal, attention to detail, and giving every song the weight it deserves in a room full of people.

Visually, the band leans into broken digital aesthetics. Their world is filled with CRT glow, glitch effects, rough textures, and corrupted screens. Their website and visuals follow the same tone as their sound—distorted, direct, and deliberately imperfect.

Producers and peers across the Sheffield scene have already taken notice. One early supporter said, "They sound like a band already two years ahead of where they're at." Their early work earned support through demo sharing, word of mouth, and pre-release gig previews. With each step, the band has refined their sound without losing what makes it urgent.

Pineapple Bandits are here to connect. They don't write background music or chase trends. They write for people who want to feel something real. Their music lives in the tension before release, in the edge of a scream, in the silence after impact.

That's where the listener meets them. And that's where they stay.
